How SolarSnap works

Four simple steps — all from your phone. No technical knowledge required.

  1. 1

    Open the app and allow location access

    When you open SolarSnap for the first time, it will ask for permission to use your GPS location. This is used to pull accurate solar irradiance data for your exact address — it isn't stored or shared.

  2. 2

    Stand at the location you want to assess

    Walk to the spot where you plan to install your solar panel — typically outside on a patio, balcony, or garden. The assessment is specific to where you physically stand, so try to be as accurate as possible.

  3. 3

    Point your camera and take a photo

    Follow the on-screen guide to aim your phone's camera towards the sky. SolarSnap analyses obstructions, shading, and your orientation relative to the sun's path to assess exposure.

  4. 4

    Read your suitability result

    Within seconds you'll receive a clear verdict: Excellent, Good, Fair, or Poor. The Basic result is free and instant. Upgrade to Premium to unlock your estimated annual energy yield in kWh and an indication of potential savings.
